Cookie Policy

How Spainza uses cookies and similar technologies.

1General provisions and scope

This Cookie Policy (the “Policy”) sets out how cookies and functionally similar local-storage technologies are used on the website spainza.com and the client portal (the “Service”) operated by Spainza (the “Operator”), the legal bases for their use, and how the User can manage them.

This Policy forms an integral part of the Privacy Policy and the Terms of Service and is to be construed together with them. Use of the Service entails the application of strictly necessary technologies as described below.

2Definitions

  • Cookie — a small text file placed in the browser on the User’s device and accessible on subsequent interactions with the Service.
  • Local storage (localStorage) — a browser mechanism for persistent storage of data on the User’s device without transmitting it with every request.
  • Session storage (sessionStorage) — a browser mechanism for temporary storage of data within a tab session.
  • Strictly necessary technologies — cookies and storage means without which the functionality requested by the User cannot be provided.

3Legal bases

The placement and reading of strictly necessary technologies is carried out on the basis of the Operator’s legitimate interest in ensuring the operability and security of the Service and for the performance of the contract with the User; under applicable law on the privacy of electronic communications (including Directive 2002/58/EC, “ePrivacy”), such technologies do not require prior consent.

Should non-strictly-necessary (analytics, preference, or marketing) technologies be introduced, they will be applied only with the User’s prior consent, expressed through an appropriate consent-management mechanism, with the ability to withdraw it subsequently.

4Technologies used and their purposes

Strictly necessary (technical):

  • access_token (HTTP cookie, typically with HttpOnly/Secure attributes and a limited validity period) — authentication in the client portal and authorised access to protected application interfaces;
  • currentUserProfile, currentUserProfileSavedAt (localStorage) — caching of profile data for correct rendering of the interface; token (localStorage) — a legacy key retained for backward compatibility;
  • spainza.language, userLocale (localStorage) — saving the selected interface language and locale.

Functional/session: short-lived sessionStorage values (in particular, manager_invite_token) used to complete specific authorisation and linking flows.

Analytics and marketing: as of the publication date, the Operator does not use any third-party advertising or analytics technologies (including Google Analytics, Meta Pixel, or similar). Should this approach change, this Policy will be updated accordingly.

5Retention periods

Session cookies cease to be valid when the authentication token expires or upon sign-out from the Account. sessionStorage values are removed when the browser tab session ends. localStorage data is retained until the User clears it via the browser or until the application clears the relevant keys (in particular, on sign-out from the Account).

6Management and opt-out

The User may restrict, block, or delete cookies and local-storage data through their browser settings. The User acknowledges and agrees that disabling strictly necessary technologies makes it impossible to sign in to the client portal and use protected features, the responsibility for which rests with the User.

Relevant instructions are usually provided under the Privacy, Security, or Cookies sections of the browser in use (Chrome, Safari, Firefox, Edge, and others).

7Third-party resources and providers

The Service may load fonts and styling libraries from content delivery networks (in particular, Google Fonts and UI library CDNs) and may use the Telegram login widget. Such providers may process technical data (including IP address and browser information) under their own policies, which the Operator does not influence and for which it is not responsible.

The User independently and at their own responsibility assesses the risks of following external links and using third-party services.

8Changes to the Policy

The Operator may unilaterally update this Policy. The effective version, stating its effective date, is posted on this page. Continued use of the Service after changes are published constitutes the User’s acceptance of the updated version.
